# Procurement and Purchasing Policy

**Document Number:** POL-CS-007  
**Version:** 3.2  
**Approved by:** Council Resolution 2023-C-145  
**Effective Date:** 1 July 2023  
**Next Review:** June 2025  
**Document Owner:** Margaret Chen, Director of Corporate Services & Finance

## 1. Policy Statement

Metro Council WA is committed to achieving value for money in all procurement activities while supporting local economic development, maintaining transparency, and upholding the highest standards of probity and ethical conduct. This policy ensures compliance with the Local Government Act 1995 (WA), Local Government (Functions and General) Regulations 1996, and Council's Strategic Community Plan 2021-2031.

All procurement activities must demonstrate accountability, transparency, competitiveness, and sustainability while considering the economic, social, and environmental impacts on our community.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all procurement of goods, services, and works by Metro Council, including:
- Direct purchases and contracts
- Panel arrangements and standing offers
- Emergency procurement
- Procurement through State Government common use arrangements
- Procurement undertaken by Council's wholly-owned subsidiaries

This policy does not apply to employment contracts, land acquisitions under the Land Administration Act, or statutory payments.

## 3. Procurement Authority and Thresholds

### 3.1 Delegation of Authority

The following procurement authority limits are established under Council's Delegations Register (updated September 2023):

| Value Range | Authority | Requirements |
|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| Up to $5,000 | All staff with budget responsibility | Single quote, best value consideration |
| $5,001 - $15,000 | Coordinators and above | Minimum two (2) written quotes |
| $15,001 - $50,000 | Managers and above | Minimum three (3) written quotes, evaluation matrix |
| $50,001 - $150,000 | Directors | Public tender or panel contract |
| $150,001 - $500,000 | CEO Helen Marchetti | Public tender, Council endorsement recommended |
| Over $500,000 | Council Resolution | Mandatory public tender process |

*Note: These thresholds exclude GST and are based on total contract value including extensions.*

### 3.2 Emergency Procurement

In genuine emergencies threatening public health, safety, or Council operations, the CEO may authorise procurement up to $100,000 without following standard processes. All emergency procurements must be reported to Council at the next ordinary meeting with justification.

## 4. Procurement Methods

### 4.1 Direct Purchase (Under $15,000)

For low-value purchases, officers may directly engage suppliers ensuring:
- Demonstration of value for money
- Compliance with Council's ethical procurement requirements
- Appropriate documentation and approval processes
- Consideration of local business preference where applicable

### 4.2 Request for Quotation ($15,000 - $150,000)

Quotation processes must include:
- Clear specification of requirements
- Minimum three (3) written quotes (where available)
- Documented evaluation criteria weighted as follows:
  - Price: 40-60%
  - Quality/capability: 25-40%
  - Local content: 5-15% (see Section 6)
  - Sustainability: 5-10%

### 4.3 Public Tender (Over $150,000)

All tenders over $150,000 must be publicly advertised on:
- Council website (mandatory minimum 14 days)
- WA Government Tenders website
- Local newspaper (for significant community impact projects)

Tender evaluation committees must include a minimum of three (3) officers, with mandatory probity training completed within the previous 24 months.

### 4.4 Panel Contracts

Council maintains pre-qualified supplier panels for:
- IT hardware and software (managed by Aiden Cross's team)
- Professional services (legal, engineering, planning)
- Construction and maintenance services
- Vehicle fleet management

Panel contracts are established through public tender processes every 2-3 years with built-in performance review mechanisms.

## 5. Evaluation Criteria

### 5.1 Standard Evaluation Framework

All procurement over $15,000 must use documented evaluation criteria including:

**Financial Considerations (40-60%)**
- Total cost of ownership
- Value for money assessment
- Financial stability of supplier
- Payment terms and conditions

**Quality and Capability (25-40%)**
- Technical compliance with specifications
- Relevant experience and references
- Staff qualifications and expertise
- Proposed methodology and timeline

**Risk Management (10-15%)**
- Insurance and liability coverage
- Occupational health and safety record
- Business continuity planning
- Cybersecurity capabilities (particularly relevant for digital transformation projects)

### 5.2 Digital Transformation Considerations

Given Council's current digital transformation program, all technology-related procurement must demonstrate:
- Integration capability with existing systems
- Data security and privacy compliance
- Accessibility standards (WCAG 2.1 AA minimum)
- Staff training and change management support

*Note: Aiden Cross must be consulted on all technology procurement over $25,000.*

## 6. Local Business Preference

### 6.1 Definition of Local Business

For the purposes of this policy, "local business" means:
- Primary place of business within Metro Council boundaries; OR
- Minimum 30% of workforce residing within Metro Council boundaries; OR
- Demonstrable local supply chain partnerships

### 6.2 Preference Application

Local businesses receive a 5-10% evaluation advantage (price equivalent) for:
- Goods and services under $150,000
- Construction and maintenance contracts under $500,000
- Professional services demonstrating local community understanding

This preference does not apply to:
- Specialist services unavailable locally
- State Government common use arrangements
- Emergency procurement
- Procurement where local preference would compromise value for money by more than 10%

### 6.3 Regional Preference

Where no suitable local businesses exist, preference extends to:
1. Perth metropolitan area businesses
2. Western Australian businesses
3. Australian businesses

## 7. Ethical Procurement and Sustainability

### 7.1 Supplier Code of Conduct

All suppliers must acknowledge and comply with Council's Supplier Code of Conduct, which requires:
- Fair employment practices and workplace safety
- Environmental responsibility
- Compliance with modern slavery legislation
- Indigenous economic participation (where applicable)
- Conflict of interest disclosure

### 7.2 Sustainable Procurement

Evaluation criteria must include environmental considerations:
- Whole-of-life environmental impact
- Waste reduction and circular economy principles
- Local sourcing to reduce transport emissions
- Energy efficiency and renewable energy use
- Packaging and disposal requirements

Margaret Chen's team maintains an approved sustainable products list updated quarterly, with input from Ben Abbott on environmental considerations.

## 8. Probity and Conflicts of Interest

### 8.1 Staff Obligations

All officers involved in procurement must:
- Complete mandatory procurement training (updated following digital transformation requirements)
- Declare any actual, potential, or perceived conflicts of interest
- Maintain confidentiality of tender information
- Not accept gifts, benefits, or hospitality from suppliers during procurement processes

### 8.2 Supplier Relations

Council maintains arm's length relationships with suppliers:
- No preferential treatment based on personal relationships
- Equal access to information for all potential suppliers
- Clear separation between procurement and contract management roles
- Regular supplier performance monitoring and feedback

## 9. Contract Management

### 9.1 Contract Documentation

All contracts over $15,000 must include:
- Clear scope of work and deliverables
- Performance indicators and review mechanisms
- Appropriate insurance and indemnity clauses
- Termination and dispute resolution procedures
- Cybersecurity and data protection requirements (where applicable)

### 9.2 Performance Monitoring

Department managers are responsible for:
- Regular supplier performance reviews
- Budget monitoring and variation control
- Compliance with contract terms and conditions
- Escalation of performance issues to Margaret Chen's team

Poor supplier performance is recorded in Council's vendor management system and considered in future procurement decisions.

## 10. Exemptions and Special Circumstances

### 10.1 State Government Arrangements

Council may utilise State Government common use arrangements without separate tender processes, including:
- WALGA preferred supplier arrangements
- State Fleet vehicle leasing
- Approved IT hardware and software panels

### 10.2 Sole Source Procurement

Direct appointment without tender may occur where:
- Only one supplier can meet technical requirements
- Intellectual property or compatibility constraints exist
- Genuine emergency circumstances prevent competitive process
- Extension of existing contracts provides demonstrable value

All sole source procurements over $50,000 require CEO approval with detailed justification.

## 11. Record Keeping and Reporting

### 11.1 Documentation Requirements

All procurement activities must maintain records including:
- Procurement decision rationale and approvals
- Tender documentation and evaluation matrices
- Supplier declarations and compliance certificates
- Contract variations and performance reviews

Records are maintained in Council's electronic document management system for minimum seven (7) years.

### 11.2 Regular Reporting

Margaret Chen provides quarterly procurement reports to Council including:
- Summary of procurement activities by value and method
- Local business participation rates
- Supplier performance issues and contract variations
- Compliance with policy requirements and exemptions granted

## 12. Training and Compliance

### 12.1 Staff Training

All officers with procurement responsibilities must complete:
- Initial procurement training within 3 months of appointment
- Probity training every 24 months
- Digital procurement system training (introduced as part of transformation program)
- Specialist training for complex procurement (legal, engineering, IT)

*Note: Training schedules have been adjusted to accommodate digital transformation workloads - some staff completions are pending.*

### 12.2 Policy Compliance

This policy is subject to:
- Annual review by Internal Audit
- External audit verification as part of annual financial audit
- Continuous improvement based on industry best practice
- Legislative compliance monitoring

## 13. Review and Amendment

This policy will be reviewed every two years or following significant legislative changes. The next scheduled review (June 2025) will incorporate lessons learned from the digital transformation program and updated State Government procurement guidelines.

Interim amendments may be made by Council resolution based on recommendations from the CEO or external audit findings.
