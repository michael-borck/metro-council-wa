# Code of Conduct for Council Employees

**Document Control Information**
- Document Number: POL-HR-001
- Version: 3.2
- Approved by: Helen Marchetti, Chief Executive Officer
- Approval Date: 15 March 2024
- Review Date: 15 March 2025
- Owner: Tony Sparks, Manager — People, Culture & Community Engagement

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical and professional standards expected of all Metro Council WA employees. It serves as a framework for decision-making and behaviour that maintains public trust, ensures political neutrality, and upholds the integrity of local government services.

This Code applies to all permanent, temporary, casual, and contract employees of Metro Council WA, including those working remotely or in hybrid arrangements as part of our digital transformation initiatives.

## 2. Our Values and Principles

Metro Council WA is committed to serving our community of 85,000 residents with integrity, transparency, and professionalism. Our core values guide all employee conduct:

- **Community First**: We prioritise the needs and interests of our community in all decisions
- **Integrity**: We act honestly, ethically, and with accountability
- **Excellence**: We strive for quality outcomes in service delivery
- **Innovation**: We embrace change and continuous improvement
- **Respect**: We treat all people with dignity and fairness

## 3. Public Trust and Community Service

### 3.1 Fundamental Obligations

As a local government employee, you are a custodian of public trust. Community members must have confidence that council services are delivered fairly, efficiently, and in their best interests. This obligation extends to all interactions, whether in person at our civic centre, through our new digital service platforms, or in the community.

### 3.2 Professional Standards

Employees must:
- Act with honesty, integrity, and diligence in all professional activities
- Perform duties competently and to the best of their ability
- Use council time, resources, and information appropriately
- Maintain professional relationships with colleagues, councillors, and community members
- Report suspected breaches of this Code through appropriate channels

### 3.3 Decision-Making Framework

When making decisions, consider:
1. Is this action in the community's best interest?
2. Would I be comfortable if this decision was made public?
3. Does this maintain the integrity of Metro Council WA?
4. Am I following established policies and procedures?

## 4. Political Neutrality

### 4.1 Impartial Service

Metro Council WA employees must remain politically neutral and provide impartial advice and services regardless of political affiliations. This is particularly important during the current period of community debate regarding state government housing density requirements.

### 4.2 Prohibited Activities

Employees must not:
- Use their position to advance personal political views or those of others
- Display political material in the workplace or on council property
- Publicly comment on political matters in their capacity as a council employee
- Use council resources for political activities
- Participate in political lobbying during work hours or using council resources

### 4.3 Election Periods

During local, state, and federal election periods, additional restrictions apply. Employees should consult with Margaret Chen, Director of Corporate Services & Finance, regarding specific election period protocols.

## 5. Conflicts of Interest

### 5.1 Definition and Recognition

A conflict of interest occurs when personal interests could compromise, or appear to compromise, professional judgment or decision-making. Conflicts can be actual, potential, or perceived.

### 5.2 Common Conflict Scenarios

- Financial interests in matters before council
- Personal relationships affecting professional decisions
- Secondary employment that conflicts with council duties
- Gifts, benefits, or hospitality that may influence decisions
- Access to confidential information for personal gain

### 5.3 Management and Disclosure

All conflicts must be disclosed immediately to your direct supervisor and registered in the Conflicts of Interest Register maintained by Corporate Services. Employees with conflicts must:
- Remove themselves from related decision-making processes
- Not access relevant confidential information
- Follow specific management strategies developed in consultation with management

*Note: The current register is transitioning to our new digital records management system. Until complete, declarations should be submitted to both the existing paper-based system and the new digital platform.*

## 6. Use of Council Resources

### 6.1 General Principles

Council resources include equipment, facilities, vehicles, telecommunications, information systems, intellectual property, and staff time. These resources are provided for legitimate council business and approved community benefit purposes only.

### 6.2 Information Technology and Digital Resources

With our ongoing digital transformation, appropriate use of technology resources is critical:

**Permitted Uses:**
- Business communications and documentation
- Professional development and training
- Limited personal use during breaks (subject to network policies)
- Remote work activities as authorised

**Prohibited Uses:**
- Personal business activities
- Inappropriate or offensive content
- Unauthorised software installation
- Sharing login credentials or system access
- Streaming or downloading non-work related content during business hours

All IT usage is monitored in accordance with our Digital Security Policy. Contact Aiden Cross, Director of Digital & Information Services, for specific technology use questions.

### 6.3 Council Vehicles and Equipment

Council vehicles and equipment must be used solely for authorised purposes. Personal use is strictly prohibited unless specifically approved in writing. All usage must be recorded in accordance with asset management procedures.

### 6.4 Telecommunications

Council-provided mobile phones and landlines are for business use. Limited personal use is acceptable provided it doesn't interfere with work duties or incur excessive costs. International calls and premium services require prior approval.

## 7. Social Media and Public Communications

### 7.1 Professional Social Media Use

Metro Council WA recognises social media as an important communication tool. Authorised social media accounts must:
- Maintain professional standards and council brand guidelines
- Provide accurate and timely information
- Engage respectfully with community members
- Follow approved content calendars and messaging frameworks

Only designated staff may post on official council social media accounts. Currently, this includes communications staff and approved community engagement personnel.

### 7.2 Personal Social Media Guidelines

When using personal social media accounts, employees must:
- Not identify themselves as Metro Council WA employees when discussing controversial topics
- Avoid commenting on council matters, especially during consultation periods
- Respect confidentiality obligations
- Not use council logos or imagery without permission
- Consider how posts might reflect on council's reputation

**Important**: Given community sensitivity around current planning policy changes, exercise particular caution when discussing housing, development, or planning matters on personal social media platforms.

### 7.3 Media Contact

All media enquiries must be directed to the Communications team or the CEO's office. Employees are not authorised to speak to media on council matters unless specifically designated and briefed.

## 8. Confidentiality and Information Management

### 8.1 Confidential Information

Employees regularly access confidential information including personal data, commercial information, legal advice, and strategic planning documents. This information must be protected and used only for legitimate council purposes.

### 8.2 Privacy Obligations

Under the Privacy Act and Freedom of Information Act, council has strict obligations regarding personal information. Employees must:
- Collect personal information only when necessary
- Store information securely (both physical and digital)
- Share information only with authorised personnel
- Follow data breach reporting procedures
- Respect individual privacy rights

### 8.3 Digital Information Security

As part of our digital transformation, new security protocols are being implemented:
- Use strong, unique passwords for all systems
- Enable multi-factor authentication where available
- Report suspicious emails or system behaviour immediately
- Keep software and security updates current
- Secure workstations when unattended

*Note: Full cybersecurity training is being rolled out in Q2 2024. Contact Aiden Cross's team for current training schedules.*

## 9. Workplace Behaviour and Relationships

### 9.1 Respectful Workplace

Metro Council WA is committed to providing a safe, respectful, and inclusive workplace free from harassment, bullying, and discrimination. All employees have the right to work in an environment that values diversity and treats people with dignity.

### 9.2 Professional Relationships

Maintain appropriate professional boundaries in all workplace relationships. This includes relationships with:
- Colleagues and supervisors
- Councillors and elected officials
- Community members and stakeholders
- Contractors and suppliers

### 9.3 Grievance and Complaint Procedures

Workplace issues should be addressed through established procedures:
1. Direct discussion with relevant parties (where appropriate and safe)
2. Consultation with immediate supervisor
3. Formal complaint through Tony Sparks, Manager — People, Culture & Community Engagement
4. External complaint mechanisms as outlined in Employee Handbook

## 10. Secondary Employment and Business Interests

### 10.1 Approval Requirements

Secondary employment or business interests that may conflict with council duties require written approval from your Director. Consider potential conflicts including:
- Time availability and performance impacts
- Competing loyalties or obligations
- Use of council knowledge or relationships
- Reputation risks to council

### 10.2 Ongoing Disclosure

Approved secondary employment must be reviewed annually or when circumstances change. New conflicts must be disclosed immediately.

## 11. Gifts, Benefits, and Hospitality

### 11.1 General Prohibition

Employees must not accept gifts, benefits, or hospitality that could influence decision-making or create obligations. This includes offers from:
- Contractors, suppliers, or service providers
- Community groups seeking council services
- Property developers or applicants
- Other stakeholders with council interests

### 11.2 Acceptable Exceptions

Minor promotional items (value under $50) or ceremonial gifts may be accepted if:
- They do not create actual or perceived obligations
- Refusal would cause offence or embarrassment
- They are reported to your supervisor
- They do not establish a pattern of acceptance

### 11.3 Reporting Requirements

All gifts, benefits, or hospitality offers (whether accepted or declined) must be recorded in the Gifts Register within five working days.

## 12. Compliance and Enforcement

### 12.1 Monitoring and Review

This Code is reviewed annually to ensure continued relevance and effectiveness. Regular compliance monitoring includes:
- Annual declaration processes
- Audit and review activities
- Training and awareness programs
- Incident investigation and resolution

### 12.2 Breach Consequences

Breaches of this Code may result in disciplinary action including:
- Counselling and additional training
- Formal warnings
- Performance management
- Termination of employment
- Referral to external authorities where appropriate

### 12.3 Reporting Breaches

Suspected breaches should be reported to:
- Your immediate supervisor
- Tony Sparks, Manager — People, Culture & Community Engagement
- Helen Marchetti, Chief Executive Officer
- External authorities (where legally required)

Anonymous reporting mechanisms are available through the Employee Assistance Program.

## 13. Support and Resources

### 13.1 Guidance and Advice

Employees seeking guidance on ethical issues or Code interpretation should contact:
- Immediate supervisor
- People, Culture & Community Engagement team
- Corporate Services team
- Employee Assistance Program (1800-xxx-xxx)

### 13.2 Training and Development

Mandatory Code of Conduct training is provided to all new employees and refresher training occurs every two years. Additional training is available on request and following significant policy updates.
